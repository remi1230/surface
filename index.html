Play with curved surfaces, write an equation f(u,v) in the x, y, z input fields
Keyboard keys:
Main:
S
Switch between Cartesian, spheric and cylindrical coordinates
E
Export ribbon to babylon or obj file format, don't work with fractalized meshes
Construct:
G
Build the lines step by step each time the key is pressed
W
Switch between building automatically the lines step by step, and stop the building
R
Reset the building of the lines step by step
Fractal:
F
Fractalize the current mesh
(
Save the mesh to "fractalize" with the current mesh
'
Switch between different orientation of the fractalized meshes with the template mesh
q
Decrease the size of the fractalized meshes
ù
Increase the size of the fractalized meshes
Inputs fields equations:
&
Switch between cos and sin in the x,y,z inputs fields equations
é
Switch between u and v in the x,y,z inputs fiels equations
;
Shortens the writing of equations in x,y,z inputs fields, for example cos(u) becomes cu
:
Extend the writing of equations in x,y,z inputs fields, for example co becomes cos(u)
$
A random equation
Camera:
+
Double the distance from the camera
-
Halve the camera distance
Colors:
D
Switch between dark and your theme
O
Switch between different types of coloring
B
Vertices coloring
"
Randomize dark theme color
H
Randomize theme color
Sliders:
l
Sets the value of the slider u either to PI in Cartesian coordinates, or to 90 for the other coordinates
m
Sets the value of the slider v either to PI in Cartesian coordinates, or to 90 for the other coordinates
7
Decrease the value of u slider by 1
8
Increase the value of u slider by 1
4
Decrease the value of v slider by 1
5
Increase the value of v slider by 1
è
Decrease the value of steps u slider by 1
_
Increase the value of steps u slider by 1
ç
Decrease the value of steps v slider by 1
à
Increase the value of steps v slider by 1
Plans:
X
Show or hide the plan X
Y
Show or hide the plan Y
Z
Show or hide the plan Z
List of functions:
cos(val)
Cosine of val. cosh works also
sin(val)
Sine of val. sinh works also
tan(val)
Tangent of val. tanh, atanh, and atantwo work also
abs(val)
Absolute value of val
exp(val)
Exponential of val
hypot(val1, val2)
Hypotenuse of val1 and val2
log(val)
Logarithm of val
logten(val)
Logarithm 10 of val
pow(val, nb)
Val to the power of nb. Works also with **, exemple 3**2 = 9
rnd()
A random number between 0 and 1
sign(val)
Returns 1 or -1 depending on the sign of val
sqrt(val)
Square root of val
fact(val)
Factorial of val
fib(val)
Fibonacci of val
mx(index, default value)
Return the value of f(x - index) with index = 1 by default, default value is returned if the index is negative, works with my and mz, useful to create a sequence like for example Un = Un-2 + Un-1
u_mod(modulo, val_to_return)
Returns either u or val_to_return (the return value if modulo different from 0, 0 by default) depending on the modulo, allows you to calculate certain values ​​only once on the modulo. Also works with v_mod
